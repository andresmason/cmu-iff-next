ABOUT THE FILM

“Bombshell becomes not just a stupendous tribute to Lamarr, but also a tribute to every brilliant woman ignored.”

- Kristy Puchko, The Nerdist


“Icon. Immigrant. Inventor.” Hollywood star Hedy Lamarr (Ziegfeld Girl, Samson and Delilah) was known as the world's most beautiful woman. However, mid-20th century social norms and her striking looks made it difficult for Lamarr to be acknowledged as the ingenious inventor she was. While her covert communication system was invented during World War II to defeat the Nazis, it was only towards the end of her life that Hedy was recognized as a pioneer whose concepts were used as the basis for secure Wifi, GPS, and Bluetooth. Directed by Alexandra Dean and produced by Susan Sarandon, Bombshell recounts Lamarr's legacy as a technological trailblazer by letting her tell her own story through never-before-heard audio tapes, as well as intimate reflections from her family, friends, and admirers.

ABOUT THE DIRECTOR

Alexandra Dean is an Emmy Award-winning journalist and producer. She produced documentaries for PBS before becoming a series and documentary producer at Bloomberg television, producing series like Innovators, Adventurs and Pursuits. She also writes about invention for Businessweek magazine. Today, she is a founding partner at Reframed Pictures.
