2018 SHORT FILM COMPETITION




ABOUT THE COMPETITION

Welcome to the 2018 Carnegie Mellon International "Faces" Film Festival Short Film Competition!


Our Short Film Competition buzzes and hums under the umbrella of the Carnegie Mellon International Festival. But we bring a lot of excitement to the Festival season on our own! Our annual competition event unites local and international filmmakers with professionals and with in-house audiences. All the eyes a filmmaker could ask for under one roof! We will shine the spotlight on those who present a unique vision and create a poignant conversation through their films.


Each year, the festival chooses a theme that focuses on a current social issue or idea. This year's theme is (In)Equality.


For more info about this year's Short Film Competition, please download our "Guidelines" document HERE



AWARDS

  • Grand Prize*: $1,000
  • Second Prize*: $500
  • Third Prize*: $250

*The Grand, Second, and Third prizes will be given to the films selected by a panel of respected filmmakers and scholars in Pittsburgh.



HIGH SCHOOL SCHOLARSHIP AWARDS

(sponsored by Point Park University)

This year we will be partnering with Point Park University once again to shine the spotlight on high school students who exhibit creativity through the use of film. The 3 best High School Student Films will be awarded scholarships from the Cinema Arts Department at Point Park University, as well as an invitation to the Short Film Competition Screening Event.


  • First Place: $15,000
  • Second Place: $10,000
  • Third Place: $5,000


REQUIREMENTS

Submission Form

Each submission must include a submission form. See Submission Process & Fee section below for different options to submit the form.

Year

Film must have been made between 2016-2018

Short Film Length

Films should be anywhere between 5 - 15 minutes in length.

Theme

Film must include but is not strictly limited to the Faces of (In)Equality theme. The theme is open for creative interpretation.

Subtitles

If in a language other than English, and/or if audio is in any way muffled, subtitles are required.

Number of Submissions

Each director can submit a maximum of 2 short films. Films must not have been submitted to the CMU International Film Festival Short Film Competition before.

Other Festival Submissions and/or Awards

If the film has been submitted to other festivals and/or received any awards, please list them with submission.

Format

  1. MP4 or MOV formats
  2. Use H.264 codec
  3. Code Rate: 5M per second
  4. Video Size: 720P (1280x720)

Deadlines

The early deadline for submission is February 1, 2018 and all films must be submitted prior to March 1, 2018. Failure to submit prior to the due date may result in rejection from admittance.

Selected submissions will be screened at the festival's Short Film Competition night and will also be posted onthe 2018 CMU Faces Official YouTube Channel.
