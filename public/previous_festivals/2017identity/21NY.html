ABOUT THE FILM

“New York, as seen by Piotr Stasik, is a whirl of passion, disappointment, and longing. This trance-like, poetical documentary film is a portrait of the city of lost people.”

- Culture.Pl

What stories lie behind the face of a stranger?

Our characters emerge from a crowd of commuters in the NYC subway – a man reminiscing on past relationships, a woman searching for a lasting love, a boy in search of solitude. A series of lost individuals come together to create this diverse mosaic of loneliness and longing in a wavering, trance-like style. Through these individual experiences, 21 x New York immerses us in the mesmerizing modern night-life of New York City. Together, we experience the universal desire for closeness, to be truly known and truly loved in an ever-expanding world of isolating technologies and societal barriers.

ABOUT THE DIRECTOR

Born in the countryside of Warsaw, Polish filmmaker Piotr Stasik majored in Sociology before attending a documentary class at Wajda School in Poland. Since dedicating himself to documentary filmmaking in 2005, he has directed eight films, including his most recent work A Diary of a Journey (2013) which won Golden Hobby-Horse of Cracow Special Mention at the Krakow Film Festival. Stasik is cofounder of the Association for Artistic Initiatives, which helps to support cultural projects in small towns across the globe, as well as being a former president of the Young Polish Filmmakers Association. His other films include Above the Pavements (2008), Andrzej Wajda: Let’s Shoot! (2009), and The Last Day of Summer, short (2011).
