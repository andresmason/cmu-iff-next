ABOUT THE FILM

“Actress Dani Nefussi gives completely believable knock-out performances in the role of both mothers, and newcomer Naomi Nero defies expectations as a broodingly intense, potentially volcanic six-footer in stiletto heels”

– Director Karen Cooper, Film Forum

Brazilian teenager Pierre, in the throes of self-discovery and expression is forced into the realization that the woman he calls mother is not in fact who she says she is. His single, working-class mom is arrested for having stolen him at birth. Thanks to the wonders of DNA, he’s returned to his biological parents: bourgeois, straight-laced and thrilled to have him back -- at least until he shows up in a zebra-print mini dress. His new family, straight-laced and conservative, reacts to Pierre’s sexuality and gender identity with confusion and shock, while he embraces it. Don’t Call Me Son is a candid view of adolescence in modern day Brazil, as our protagonist struggles with the loss of one family and the gain of another amidst his own discovery of self.

ABOUT THE DIRECTOR

Anna Muylaert was born in São Paulo Brazil in 1964. A graduate in Filmmaking from The University of São Paulo, she began her career as an assistant director on feature films such as Running Out of Luck (Julien Temple, 1987) and Moon Over Parador (Paul Mazursky, 1988). She is a notable screenwriter, and has done everything from directing, producing, to film criticism. Her first feature film Durval Records was released in 2001 and received 7 awards at Gramado film Festival, and was featured at many other festivals. Her previous film The Second Mother (2015) won the Panorama Audience Award at the 2015 Berlin International Film Festival and, after winning several other awards, was selected as Brazil's submission for the Academy Awards.
