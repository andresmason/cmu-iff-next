ABOUT THE FILM

The final film, Afterimage by illustrious Polish filmmaker Andrzej Wajda charts the passionate life of avant-garde artist and teacher Władysław Strzemiński. Considered a peer of Kandinsky and a pioneer of the Polish Constructivist avant-garde of the 1920s and ’30s, Strzemiński clashed with an autocratic Stalinist system, putting his own life and that of his daughter in danger. Strzemiński rose to prominence in the interwar years. Having lost an arm and a leg in the First World War he took up the brush and was an internationally acclaimed painter and theoretician by the time he ran afoul of the Communist government of the 1950s.

ABOUT THE DIRECTOR

Andrzej Wajda is one of Poland’s most respected filmmakers. He was born in Suwałki, Poland in 1926 and has directed many critically acclaimed films since the 1950s. He is well known for his war film trilogy A Generation (1954), Kanal (1956), and Ashes and Diamonds (1958) as well as for receiving Academy Award nominations for Promised Land (1975), The Maids of Wilko (1979), Man of Iron (1981), and Katyn (2007). The CMU IFF hosted his film Wałęsa: Man of Hope (2013) for its 2015 Festival season “Faces of Work.” Wajda died in 2016 at the age of 90.
