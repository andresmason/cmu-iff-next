2019 SHORT FILM COMPETITION


To learn more about how to submit your short film, see our “Submission Process” section below for options and detailed instructions.



ABOUT THE COMPETITION

Welcome to 2019 Carnegie Mellon International “Faces” Film Festival Student Short Film Competition!

Our Short Film Competition buzzes and hums under the umbrella of the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival. But we bring a lot of excitement to the Festival season on our own! Our annual competition event unites local and international filmmakers with professionals and with in-house audiences. All the eyes a filmmaker could ask for under one roof. We will shine the spotlight on those who present a unique vision and create poignant conversation through their films.

Each year, the festival chooses a theme that focuses on a current social issue or idea. This year’s theme is WO/MEN. For more info about this year’s Short Film Competition, please download our “Guidelines” document HERE
.



AWARDS

Grand Prize: $1,000
Second Prize: $500
Third Prize: $250



REQUIREMENTS

Eligibility
Film must be from Pittsburgh students and the filmmaker must be present at the screening event in order to be receive the prize. Film must not include copyrighted images, video or music.

Year
Film must have been made between 2017-2019.

Short Film Length
Films should be anywhere between 5 - 15 minutes in length.

Theme
Film must include but is not strictly limited to the Faces of WO/MEN theme. The theme is open for creative interpretation.

Subtitles
If in a language other than English, and/or if audio is in any way muffled, subtitles are required.

Number of Submissions
Each director can submit a maximum of 2 short films. Film must not have been submitted to CMU International Film Festival Short Film Competition before.

Other Festival Submissions and/or Awards
If the film has been submitted to other festivals and/or received any awards, please list them with submission.

Format
1. MP4 or MOV formats
2. Use H.264 codec
3. Code Rate: 5M per second
4. Video size: 720P (1280x720)

Deadlines
The early deadline for submission is March 1, 2019. Failure to submit prior to the due date may result in rejection from admittance.



SUBMISSION PROCESS

Submissions for this year's short film competition are FREE and will all be made through FilmFreeway.

Please include Pittsburgh, United States within the address.


SCREENING EVENT

Selected submissions will be screened at the festival’s Short Film Competition night on Saturday, April 6 at 2pm at Regent Square Theater.



CONTACT

Please email cmusfc2019@gmail.com or Jolanta Lion at jola@cmu.edu for other payment methods, submission details, or any other questions concerning the Short Film Competition.
