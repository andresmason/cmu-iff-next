Push

Directed by Fredrik Gertten

Sweden | 2019 | 92 min

About the Film

“[Push] speaks to us on a human level… What is a city, and who is meant to live there? Where will people go when they lose their homes, their communities? And how can we live with ourselves when literally trillions of dollars worth of real estate sits vacant while people die sleeping in the streets?” Jamie Taylor, Cinema Axis

Why, in so many cities around the world, are house prices skyrocketing while incomes are not? Why are so many people being pushed from their neighborhoods and homes? And what can be done to reverse such trends? Leilani Farha, the United Nations’ Special Rapporteur on Adequate Housing, travels the globe tirelessly investigating these issues and urging governments and developers to treat housing primarily as a human right, not a medium for the pursuit of private profit. Fredrik Gertten’s agile documentary follows her on her trips, highlighting both the problems she helps illuminate and the inventive ways people are trying to address them at the local level and beyond.

About the Director

Fredrik Gertten, Director is an award-winning director and journalist based in Malmö, Sweden, and owner and manager of the production company WG Film which he founded in 1994. In October 2017 he was named Honorary Doctor at Malmö University’s Faculty of Culture and Society, for his work as a documentary filmmaker.

Pittsburgh Premiere Virtual Screening!

Tickets for this event are no longer on sale.

+ On Thursday, October 29, CMU IFF emulated our in-person festival event by hosting a screening followed by a live discussion:

@ 5pm - View an online screening of "Push"

@ 7pm - The festival hosted a free live discussion on Zoom with Leilani Farha, a Canadian human rights lawyer and the main subject of the film "Push". Farha spoke about her work bringing demands for housing justice to governments and working with the global housing rights movement through her organization #Maketheshift. The discussion was facilitated by Rob Robinson, an organizer with the US Human Rights Cities Alliance, International Alliance of Inhabitants, and Partners for Dignity and Rights.
Other panelists included:
Carl Redwood - Hill District Consensus Group. (Pittsburgh)
Dr. Yolande Tomlinson - Organization for Human Rights and Democracy (Atlanta).
