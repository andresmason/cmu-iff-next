Out Stealing Horses

Directed by Hans Petter Moland

Norway/Sweden/Denmark | 2020 | 123 min

Details
Showtimes

About the Film

“Moland’s film could hardly be more beautiful. It captures the essence of a long-gone summer when everything looked, smelled – and was – good. It encapsulates the cold, the darkness, the harried moments, and the melancholy of bygone days and bygone chances. The cast, jam-packed with Scandinavian acting nobility, shine.” Jan Lumholdt, Cineuropa

Based on the 2003 award-winning novel by Per Petterson, Out Stealing Horses weaves together the past and present of Trond and Lars, childhood neighbors who reunite in rural Norway as old men. Switching effortlessly between the 1940s and the late 1990s, the film shows how Trond, Lars, and Lars’ older brother, Jon, deal with loss, tragic accidents, and adolescent desire during their time together in the summer of 1948 and how Trond and Lars process their shared but divergent experiences five decades later. How have the two men been marked by the complexities of family, manhood, love, and grief? And what happens to their feelings as they trade their memories and remake them?

About the Director

Hans Petter Moland was born in Norway in 1955. He gained widespread recognition for his commercials before releasing his first feature film, The Second Lieutenant, in 1993. He has since made eleven films, including Aberdeen (2000), The Beautiful Country (2004), A Somewhat Gentle Man (2010), In Order of Disappearance (2014), and the prize-winning short, United We Stand (2002). Many of his works have been screened and won awards at major festivals around the world.
