"I Do Not Care If We Go Down In History As Barbarians"

Directed by Radu Jude

Romania/Germany/Bulgaria/France/Czech Republic | 2018 | 140 min

Details

About the Film

“…a dazzlingly dialectical and daring comedy/drama that skilfully brings past and present together and again challenges Jude’s [Romanian] compatriots to face up to the more unsavoury aspects of their history.” Demetrios Matheou, Screen Daily

What myths do people perpetuate and what truths do they suppress in order to maintain commitment to an idealized national homeland? And what can be done to encourage a franker interrogation of the past and its relation to the present? Radu Jude’s startling black comedy follows a Romanian theater director as she prepares a one-off spectacle designed to highlight her country’s active involvement in the extermination of Jews during much of the Second World War. Combining dark humor and deadly serious debate, it shows her struggling constantly to deal with the obstacles thrown up by both government officials and people hired to perform in the event. In the process, it presses us to think hard not only about a ghastly moment in Romania’s past and the broader horrors of fascist ideology and practice but also about the resurgent force of authoritarian nationalism in so many regions of the world today.

About the Director

Born in Romania in 1977, Radu Jude graduated from the Media University of Bucharest’s Film Directing Department in 2003. He directed several award-winning short films before releasing in 2009 his first feature film, The Happiest Girl in the World, which garnered prizes at numerous international festivals. Since then he has directed and co-written a series of widely acclaimed films, including Everybody in Our Family (2012), the historical drama Aferim! (2015), and Scarred Hearts (2016). These have made him a leading figure in the stellar group of filmmakers that has emerged in Romania during the last two decades.
