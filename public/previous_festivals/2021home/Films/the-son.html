Directed by Ines Tanović

Bosnia and Herzegovina | 2019 | 106 min

Showtimes

About the Film

What happens when people’s homes and homelands are both riven by deep and lasting tensions? Set in Bosnia and Herzegovina some two decades after the conclusion of its civil war, The Son follows troubled teenager Arman as he struggles to deal with the difficulties of being adopted as well as continuing rejection by the woman who bore him. In a country still scarred by the marks of brutal ethnic conflict, will he be able to find comfort in an inter-ethnic relationship with a young Serbian woman? And will he be able to protect his younger stepbrother and, indeed, himself from the persistent lure of drugs, guns, and fighting in the Middle East?

About the Director

Ines Tanović was born in Sarajevo in 1965. She studied dramaturgy there before moving into writing and directing. After making a number of short and documentary films, she gained widespread attention for her work on the Bosnian part of the feature omnibus Some Other Stories (2010) and for her debut feature film, Our Everyday Life (2015), which was Bosnia and Herzegovina’s candidate for Best Foreign Language Film at the 2015 Academy Awards. She is currently president of the Association of Film Workers of Bosnia and Herzegovina.
