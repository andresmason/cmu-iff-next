Our Mothers (Nuestras Madres)

Directed by César Díaz

Belgium/Guatemala | 2019 | 78 min

Details
Showtimes

About the Film

How can one ever feel at home in a country where many of one’s family members were victims of a systematic slaughter and where most of the people who ordered, financed, and carried out the killings have not been brought to justice? Ernesto is a young forensic anthropologist working to identify the bodies of people thrown into mass graves during the Guatemalan genocide that extended from the 1960s to the mid-1990s. He hopes to provide some relief for the surviving family members, many of them women, but, in the process, he comes across a lead that may bring the whole matter much closer to home both for him and for his widowed mother.

About the Director

César Díaz was born in Guatemala in 1978. After studying in Mexico and Belgium, he joined the screenwriting workshop at La Fémis, the famous film and television school in Paris. He has been working as a fiction and documentary film editor for more than ten years. He has also directed the short documentary films Semillas de Cenizas, which was screened in about twenty international film festivals, and Territorio Liberado, which won an award from the Mexican Film Institute. Our Mothers is his first feature film.
