special guests



DANUTA WAŁĘSA


Wife of Lech Wałęsa and character in Wałęsa: Man of Hope

Danuta Wałęsa, the former First Lady of Poland, published her first autobiography, Dreams and Secrets, in 2011. Her frank memoir, which details the struggles she experienced as her husband, Nobel Peace Prize-winning Polish Solidarity leader Lech Wałęsa, rose to power, caused a stir in Poland even before its official release, and it later inspired her depiction in Andrzej Wajda's award-winning film, Wałęsa: Man of Hope (2013). While her husband was leading the fight to topple communism in Poland in 1989 with his Solidarity trade union movement and serving as Poland's first democratically elected president from 1990 to 1995, Danuta was fighting for her family and the loneliness and domestic grind she faced while raising their eight children.

Ralph Vituccio


Director of Shipbreakers

Ralph Vituccio is the Director of Media Development in Communications Design and an instructor in the Entertainment Technology Center at Carnegie Mellon. He is also an adjunct faculty member at Pittsburgh Filmmakers, where he teaches film and video production. He has developed, written, and produced numerous award-winning films, videos, CD-ROMs, DVD-ROMs, websites, and multimedia projects. Vituccio's documentary, PERFORMANCE: The Living Art, received an Artist Distinction Award at the 1990 Berlin International Film Festival and has aired nationally on several PBS stations and internationally in several countries.

SHOSH SHLAM


Co-director of Web Junkie

Shosh Shlam is an Israeli filmmaker who has produced and directed award-winning documentaries for over 10 years. Her films have received critical acclaim at film festivals around the world, including Journey into the Silence (2001) and Good Garbage (2012), which, respectively, received the Best Documentary Awards at the Lublin Film Festival in Poland and at the FICA Film Festival in Brazil. Shlam most recently co-directed the documentary Web Junkie (2013), which premiered at this year's Sundance Film Festival.

AdriÁn Saba


Director of The Cleaner

Adrián Saba is a Spanish-born filmmaker who earned a film degree from Hofstra University on a Fulbright Scholarship in 2010. His first feature film, The Cleaner (El Limpiador), has been shown at festivals around the world, winning accolades such as the New Voices/New Visions Grand Jury Prize at the 2013 Palm Springs International Film Festival and the Jury's Special Mention at the 2012 San Sebastian Film Festival. The Cleaner was also selected as Peru's 2014 Oscar entry for the 'Best Foreign Language Film' category. In 2011, Saba founded his own production company called Flamingo Films. He is currently developing his next screenplay as a fellow in the highly selective Cinefondation Residence Program, which is organized by the Cannes Film Festival.

ANDRES TAPIA-URZUA


Director of Open City of Amereida

Andres Tapia-Urzua/"ATU" is an artist practicing in video, music, performance, interactivity, installation and cultural theory. His work continually explores the liminality of a cross-cultural, cross-technological identity. Fusing aesthetics, theory and political issues, he offers us a poignant and fascinating look at cultural identity –where social alienation, high-tech environments and the poetics of globalization are represented in sharp and accessible ways. Andres demonstrates a commanding presence in his original videos that embrace the politics of the personal and how the personal becomes political through an often surreal, dream-like poetic.

Nego Gato


Guest artist for GriGris premiere

Jose Sena, known professionally as Nego Gato, is the Founder, and Artistic and Executive Director of Nego Gato, Inc., a Pittsburgh based Afro-Brazilian Music and Dance Ensemble. The Ensemble is renowned for its work in arts education, and has won several awards, including the PA Health Department's Violence Free Challenge Award for its contributions to The Ginga Project, an initiative to prevent violence through the arts. Since 1989 Nego Gato has empowered audiences and brought educational opportunities to schools and community organization through vibrant African Brazilian influenced rhythms, dances, and historical narratives, and are currently on the roster of Gateway to the Arts of Pittsburgh and Young Audiences of New York.

Heather Arnet


Director of Madame Presidenta: Why Not U.S.?—Vamos Meninas!

Heather Arnet is a tireless advocate for women and girls' rights locally, nationally, and internationally. In her role as CEO of the Women and Girls Foundation, she is a regular Op-Ed contributor to the Pittsburgh Post-Gazette, Daily Beast and Huffington Post and on-air commentator to local and national media. Arnet has appeared on numerous television programs and in 2014, she was identified as "One of the 21 Leaders for a New Century" by Women's Enews. Arnet is a member of the Dramatist Guild of America and has her BA degree from Carnegie Mellon University in English and Drama (with major concentration on Directing). Arnet has worked for "Mister Rogers' Neighborhood" and for several advertising and production agencies in New York as a freelance television/video producer and director. Arnet is also an accomplished theater director: Recently she directed the world premiere of Vanessa German's root. Other works include Yo'Mama!, Joyce Carol Oates' I Stand Before You Naked, Multiple Satellite Media Tours for Can Do Woman Productions, and Oscar Wilde's Salome. 

PARVIZ SHAHBAZI


Director of Trapped

Parviz Shahbazi is an acclaimed Iranian filmmaker who has been writing and directing shorts and feature films since the 1980s. After graduating from Tehran's Iran Broadcasting University, he gained international attention after debuting award-winning short films like Traveler from the South (1996) and Whispers (2000), in addition to his first feature-length film, Deep Breath (2003). His latest film, Trapped (2013), has been a favorite of audiences and critics alike at international film festivals such as the Vancouver International Film Festival and the Fajr International Film Festival in Iran, where Shahbazi received the Best Director Award.

LISA FRUCHTMAN


Co-director of Sweet Dreams

Lisa Fruchtman is a dynamic filmmaker who has directed, produced, and edited a number of acclaimed documentaries, television programs, and feature films over the years. She won the 1984 Academy Award for Best Film Editing for her work on The Right Stuff (1983), and she was an Academy Award nominee for her editing work on The Godfather: Part III (1990) and Apocalypse Now (1979). Furthermore, she has also edited a number of compelling HBO films, including Witness Protection (1999), Point of Origin (2002), Normal (2003), and the Emmy-nominated Truman (1995). Lisa and her brother, fellow filmmaker Rob Fruchtman, share a personal and professional interest in issues surrounding the arts, global cultures, and social justice, which are reflected in the latest documentary, Sweet Dreams (2013).

ALLEN LU


Producer of Linsanity

Allen Lu graduated from Carnegie Mellon with a B.S. in Information Systems, and he is currently an eCommerce entrepreneur with a focus in sports and entertainment. While working for American Eagle Outfitters, Lu launched two brands online, MartinandOsa.com and 77kids.com. After developing an eCommerce brand strategy for GNC, he also worked as VP of Technology, Supply Chain, and Distribution at ModCloth.com. Lu now consults for various retail and technology startups while also developing business opportunities for NBA player Jeremy Lin.
