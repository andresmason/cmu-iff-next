Song from the Forest

Directed by Michael Obert | 2013 | Germany/Congo | 96 min.



"Song From the Forest succeeds in being a striking and gently haunting film." Mark Adams, SCREEN INTERNATIONAL

How much of the world have you never known? Years ago, Louis Sarno, an American musicologist, traveled to the rainforests of Central Africa to study the culture and music of the Bayaka Pygmies, an African tribe of hunters and gatherers. Enraptured by their rhythm, he turned away from the industrialized life he had always known and immersed himself in the world of the Bayaka-- their music, language and families. Flash forward a quarter century. Sarno steps out of the forest and, with his Bayaki son, into the concrete jungle of his previous life. Songs From the Forest forms a duet between one world of flashing lights and glass skyscrapers and another of earth rhythms and wild fauna, weaving together two different spheres into one broad picture of life and music.

Director

German journalist and travel writer Michael Obert has long been based in Africa and the Middle East. His work regularly appears in European newspapers including the Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung (Germany) and the Sunday Times Magazine (England). He is the author of several books, including Regenzauber (On the River of Gods) – an account of a seven-month journey along the River Niger in Africa – and Die Rȁnder der Welt (The Edges of the World) – which includes war zones in Afghanistan, the Sudan, and Nigeria as well as more tourist-friendly locations. Song from the Forest is Obert's first documentary film.
