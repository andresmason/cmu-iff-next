Dangerous Acts

Directed by Madeleine Sackler | 2013 | Belarus | 76 min.



"Sackler's engrossing documentary chronicles upheaval in 'the last dictatorship in Europe' through the viewpoint of an underground theatre company." WNYC, The Leonard Lopate Show

In Belarus where loved ones disappear, elections are rigged against the popular vote, and the people are silenced, a team of eight has been branded a political enemy of Europe's last dictator. The cause? Their acts of rebellion, executed in the form of balloons, a cigarette, and plastic wrap. Belarus Free Theatre is an underground theater company in Minsk, giving voice to taboo subjects and telling truths to secret audiences for five years until, in 2010, during a rigged presidential election, the group was branded as a threat to the dictatorship of Alexander Lukashenko. Using smuggled and uncensored footage of Belarus protests, Dangerous Acts Starring The Unstable Elements of Belarus illustrates the risk these exiled artists take on, as they keep alive their resistance against the oppression of their homeland--a reminder of the power that fearless speech and public art can exert even within "the last dictatorship in Europe."

Director

New York-based producer and director Madeleine Sackler founded Great Curve Films, which has since been merged into Madbrook Films with actor and director Boyd Holbrook. Her most recent documentary, Dangerous Acts Starring the Unstable Elements of Belarus, has been acquired by HBO. Her first film, The Lottery, sparked a renewed debate on the future of public education and was shortlisted for the 2011 Academy Awards.
