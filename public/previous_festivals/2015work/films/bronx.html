Bronx Obama

Directed by Ryan Murdock | 2013 | USA | 91 min.



"Funny and Insightful" Variety

"Murdock captures each moment with a quiet and measured eye, blending the pathos of Ortiz's personal journey with moments of humor that highlight the unreal nature of his dual life." Sarah Walsh, Vox Magazine

The American Dream comes in many guises, not all of them bringing unalloyed benefits. When Louis Ortiz shaved off his goatee one day in 2008, his life changed forever. He looked in the mirror and he didn't see himself – a middle-aged, unemployed Puerto Rican father from the Bronx. He saw the face of change, of hope… of money. His uncanny resemblance to the new Democratic Party candidate – soon to be President – Barack Obama – opened a totally unexpected new 'career' for Ortiz, whose fortunes changed overnight. He won a shadow-fame and, if not a fortune, at least a gig – as an Obama impersonator. But Ortiz discovers the pitfalls and uncertainties of a show business career, and struggles with both the consequences in his private life. Director Ryan Murdock captures unexpectedly hilarious moments along this Twilight Zone-like campaign trail while delving deep into the question of what it means to be someone you're not.

Awards

  • AFI Docs Film Festival, "Best of Fest" Award winner

Director

Producer/Director Ryan Murdock is an independent filmmaker and the creative director of his company, Saving Daylight Productions, which creates documentary media for clients and develops original editorial projects. His recent work has been featured on the BBC, The New York Times, MSNBC, The Huffington Post, and Gawker, among others. Bronx Obama is Ryan's first feature length documentary. He adapted his story for an episode of This American Life and as a short for The New York Times. The New York Times said Murdock "defies categorization," which he considers a compliment.
