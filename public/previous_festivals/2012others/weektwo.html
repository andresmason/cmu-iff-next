The 2012 Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival: Faces of Others

WEEK TWO!


Regular Admission: $5 seniors and students | $8 'Others'
Full Access Festival Pass: $25 seniors and students | $50 'Others'

Tickets available at the door or at cmu.edu/faces.

Putin's Kiss (Denmark/Russia, 2011)
Thursday, March 29, 7:00 p.m. -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University

19-year-old Masha Drokova, also known as "the girl who kissed Putin," is the deeply involved spokesperson for Nashi, a Russian Youth organization that promotes strong nationalistic ideals and seeks to rid Russia of its 'enemies'. However, as Masha grows and begins to befriend members of the liberal opposition, her beliefs are challenged.

Features a lively discussion moderated by Jonathan Harris, Professor of Political Science at the University of Pittsburgh!

Girl with Black Balloons (USA/Netherlands, 2011)
Thursday, March 29, 7:15 p.m. -- Harris Theater, 809 Liberty Avenue

Girl with Black Balloons follows first-time director Corinne van der Borch into the Chelsea Hotel in NYC where she befriends an eccentric artist by the name of Bettina. Full of humorous and touching moments, the film portrays an amazing glimpse into the world of one of the Chelsea's most mysterious residents.

Director Corinne van der Borch will introduce her film and host a panel discussion following the screening!  The event will also kick off the "Distinctively Dutch" Film Festival with an opening reception featuring authentic Dutch food specialties and Heineken beer!

Joschka and Sir Fischer (Germany/Switzerland, 2011)
Friday, March 30, 6:30 p.m. -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University

Through never-before-seen footage and in-person interviews with his contemporaries, Joschka and Sir Fischer recounts the extraordinary biography of colorful German politician Joschka Fischer and his role in shaping 60 years of post-World War II German history.

The film's Oscar award-winning director Pepe Danquart will host a Q&A session with the audience! The discussion with be moderated by two professors from the University of Pittsburgh: Sabine von Dirke, Professor of German, and Gregor Thum, Professor of History.  

Position Among the Stars (Netherlands, 2010)
Saturday, March 31, 2:00 p.m. -- Harris Theater, 809 Liberty Avenue

"Engrossing, poetic, and often very funny, Position Among the Stars, like its predecessors, uses the lens of a single family to view the tumult of an entire country." -- New York Times

Features a Skype session with the film's director, Leonard Retel Helmrich!

Alps (Greece, 2011)
Saturday, March 31, 5:30 p.m. -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University

Alps is a mysterious and unique organization where its members take on roles of the dearly departed in order to help relatives deal with the loss of their loved ones. Alps explores the individual struggles of its characters as they mimic the dead, and reveals the human race's often desperate desire for a part to play and a place to fit in.

A Greek food reception will follow the screening!

El Sicario, Room 164 (France/USA, 2011)
Saturday, March 31, 7:30 p.m. -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University

El Sicario, Room 164 explores the 'terrifyingly casual roots of evil' through the eyes of an unnamed former "sicario" - a hit man and enforcer for a drug cartel in Ciudad Juarez who once kidnapped and tortured someone in the very room in which Gianfranco Rosi films this thrilling documentary.

The screening will be followed by a riveting discussion moderated by Paul Eiss, Director of the Center for the Arts in Society at Carnegie Mellon University!

Mum (Netherlands, 2009)
Sunday, April 1, 6:30 p.m. -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University

"Now that my mother has developed Alzheimer's disease, I don't see her dissolving, I see her appearing." In her originally stylized documentary, director Adelheid Roosen paints an intimate and private portrait of her mother. Replete with tenderness and humor, Mum undertakes a journey into the world of a woman with an elusive and debilitating disease.

Director Adelheid Roosen will make a special appearance! This event is FREE and open to the public!

Every year, the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival challenges its participants to view the world through a myriad of multicultural lenses. In 2012, the tradition continues with this year's theme: Faces of Others. The Other often refers to strange faces, practices, and beliefs that challenge our understanding, security, and sense of normalcy, but at the same time, the Other is something that every person on this planet experiences. The festival invites you to explore the concept of the Other and join the minds of numerous national cinematic traditions.
