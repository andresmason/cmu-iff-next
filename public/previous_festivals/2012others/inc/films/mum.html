“Now that my mother has developed Alzheimer’s disease, I don’t see her dissolving, I see her appearing. I see her as an Alice in Wonderland: she is falling through time. I fall after her, discover where she is, what she is going through, what she is doing or saying.” In her originally stylized documentary, director Adelheid Roosen paints an intimate and private portrait of her mother. Replete with tenderness and humor, Mum undertakes a journey into the world of a woman with an elusive and debilitating disease.

Adelheid Roosen is a Dutch director, writer, teacher, and actress who has won awards for her performances in the play Waking Up Painstakingly Slowly, and in the film Vulnerable. As a director and writer, Roosen is known for her straightforward and sometimes controversial and taboo-breaking statements. These elements are reflected in two of her theater productions, The Veiled Monologues and Is.Man, which reveal intimate portrayals of Muslim men and women living in Holland and which have toured through the United States, Turkey, and France. Roosen is currently editor-in-chief of Zina Platform, a means for expression for Arabian, Turkish, and Kurd artists, an advisor and lecturer at the Amsterdam Theatre School, and a mentor at Das Arts.
