CMU's "Faces of Globalization" International Film Festival
is proud to present

Friday, April 9 at 8:00 PM
McConomy Audorium, CMU

If the gravity of a totalitarian regime seems ill fitting as the subject for a musical, Yodok Stories is mind changing. Inspired by the theatrics of the communist parades he once filmed in North Korea, acclaimed Polish Director Andrzej Fidyk conceived of the idea to portray the conflict of the split nation in a musical. North Korean defector, Jung Sung San took the bait, risked his life and began the intense theater-directing process of putting his painful memories of the Yodok concentration camp to music and dance.

Admission: $7 / $4 Students
Purchase tickets at the door or online at cmu.edu/faces

Featuring food from Ginza Korean & Japanese Restaurant and local Korean students. Plus, the following panelists will be making a special appearance:

Attilio Favorini - (Ph.D., Yale University) has served as the founding Chair of the Department of Theatre Arts at the University of Pittsburgh and the Founder and Producing Director of the Three Rivers Shakespeare Festival (1980-1992). He is currently Professor and Director of Graduate Studies in Theatre Arts at the University of Pittsburgh.

Seunghwan Shin - is a graduate student in the University of Pittsburgh Graduate School of Arts and Sciences studying English, Film Studies, and Cultural Studies. He is currently teaching Introduction to Korea through Film at the University of Pittsburgh.

Rev. Dr. Timothy Son - received his bachelor's from Cornell University, his M.Div. and Th.M. from Princeton Theological Seminary, and completed his doctoral studies at Teachers College, Columbia University. He is currently serving as a director of the 'Con.fluence' research project, a three-year project of discerning critical issues of ministry in Asian American congregations.
