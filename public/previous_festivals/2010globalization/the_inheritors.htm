Directed by Eugenio Polgovsky; Mexico, 2009; 90 min

Pittsburgh Premiere

"An impressive depiction of the bleak lives of Mexico's rural population... reminiscent of the early socio-critical works of directors such as Jean Rouch and Fernando Birri."  -Berlin International Film Festival

Awards

* 2009 Silver Arial Award (Mexico) for Best Editing and
Best Feature Length Documentary
* 2009 Lima Latin American Film Festival Award for Best Documentary
From stumbling toddlers to blind elders, everyone must work for these rural Mexican families to exist.  Despite the extreme working conditions and rugged terrains, the people exude a lightness of spirit.  The director presents little dialogue and an unbiased observation of a world far from the advances of technology.

Eugenio Polgovsky

Born in Mexico City in 1977, Polgovsky went on to study directing and cinematography at the Centro de Capacitación Cinematografíca.  In 1994, he won the UNESCO organized, world photography contest “Living together.” His first documentary, Tropic of Cancer, won several prizes around the world (an Ariel Award for Best First Documentary by the Mexican Academy of Cinematography, the Joris Ivens Prize at Cinema du Réel, Best Documentary in Lebanon, Korea, Morelia and FICCO). Cannes Film Festival featured a special screening of Tropic of Cancer during the Semaine de la critique and was part of Frontier Selection at Sundance.  The Inheritors is his latest documentary and premiered at the 65th Venice Film Festival.
