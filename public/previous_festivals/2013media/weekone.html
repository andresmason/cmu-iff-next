The We and the I (USA, 2012)
Thursday, March 21, 7:15pm -- Melwood Screening Room, 477 Melwood Avenue



Click here for more information.
Opening Night Reception !

The We and the I follows a group of Bronx high schoolers who board a city bus on their way home on the last day of school. With summer break ahead of them, and the group feeling more liberated than usual, the bus soon becomes a micro-community where their friendships, rivalries, ambitions, and anxieties are revealed. Though set almost entirely on the bus, director Michel Gondry enlarges the film's visual canvas with mobile phone videos, text messages, and a series of trompe l'oeil effects executed with his customary flair and inventiveness.

Director Michel Gondry will introduce his film via Skype! The event will also feature an Opening Night Reception.

Crocodile in the Yangtze* (China/USA, 2012)
Friday, March 22, 7:00pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University 



*A Goodman Faces of Work Film

Click here for more information.
Goodman Faces of Work Opening Reception !

Crocodile in the Yangtze shares the inspiring story of renowned Chinese entrepreneur Jack Ma and the challenges and controversies he and his company, Alibaba Group, encountered as Alibaba grew from a small apartment into China's first global Internet company employing more than 16,000 people.

Preceded by 'Goodman Faces of Work' Opening Reception with Chinese food specialties, and a screening of Dr. Paul Goodman's short film CERT!  A discussion with director Porter Erisman will follow the film!

Suspiciously in Love (Poland, 2013)
Saturday, March 23, 5:00pm -- Melwood Screening Room, 477 Melwood Avenue


Click here for more information.
Suspiciously in Love follows Albert, a shy young man who stumbles upon Basia in his search to find love online.  Each employs the same dating strategy - Albert poses as a handsome French beau while Basia claims to be a mysterious woman names Simone.  The pair maintains this facade even on their first date in 'real life' where both send an attractive replacement to act on their behalf.  Their plans quickly unravel as the group becomes entangled with a gang of thieves hunting for a priceless collection of diamonds.  In this farcical comedy of errors and surprising twists, nobody knows who is in love and who is suspect!

A favorite at the 24th Polish Film Festival in Chicago and at the Cantor Film Center in New York!

InContact (Israel/USA, 2012)
Saturday, March 23, 7:00pm -- Melwood Screening Room, 477 Melwood Avenue


Click here for more information.
InContact depicts the interconnected lives of Gen and Christina, two young women who connect through the online social platform "InContact", which broadcasts its users' daily experiences over a series of live video feeds. Director Ann Oren invites the audience into this voyeuristic world as we, like the characters, sit back and watch as tensions rise and drama unfolds though the eyes of social media.

Features a discussion with director Ann Oren and a food reception compliments of the Allegro Hearth Bakery!

Tahrir - Liberation Square (Italy/France, 2011)
Sunday, March 24, 3:00pm -- Melwood Screening Room, 477 Melwood Avenue


Click here for more information. 
Tahrir: Liberation Square follows a group of young Egyptians during two of the most exciting weeks in the history of modern Egypt. Together with thousands of other Egyptian citizens, they come to the Square chanting, marching, and protesting to overthrow Mubarak's regime of oppression, corruption and abuse. Through riveting moments of hope, despair, anger, and pride, this compelling documentary uncovers the unexpected heroes behind the Egyptian revolution, including Google executive Wael Ghonim whose Facebook page helped spark this massive movement for freedom.

A panel discussion with professors from the University of Pittsburgh will follow the film screening! 

Sushi: The Global Catch* (USA/Poland/Japan/Australia, 2011)
Sunday, March 24, 5:30pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


*A Goodman Faces of Work Film

Click here for more information. 
Sushi: The Global Catch guides us through the fascinating world of the global sushi phenomenon.  While creating an astonishing visual feast, director Mark Hall also raises concerns about the industry's sustainability and its potential to disturb the ecological balance of the world's oceans.  This documentary is a delicious, thought-provoking exploration of the state and future of the global sushi industry.

Dr. Paul Goodman's short film Lobstermen will precede the featured film!  The event will also feature a Japanese reception.

 
Seven years since its inception, the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival continues to celebrate the art of filmmaking and the themes that define our contemporary social landscape. Through the power of the independent film, the 2013 Carnegie Mellon Film Festival: Faces of Media brings the intricacies surrounding media to life. These films both move us and enrich us by helping us better comprehend the unique ways in which media outputs shape our modern world.
