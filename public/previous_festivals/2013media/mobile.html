 We hope to see your faces in the audience!

"A hard-hitting documentary that shows us the appalling price paid in Africa to sate our obsession for mobile phones." - The Guardian UK

Mainstream film introduced us to the corrupt world of the blood diamond industry in Africa, but rarely do we hear about the equally coveted African conflict minerals that hide in our cell phones and other favorite electronics. In Blood in the Mobile, Danish filmmaker Frank Poulsen journeys down the mines in the eastern DR Congo to investigate the illegal coltan and cassiterite mining industry that is financing the country's civil war. His shocking revelation leads him on a campaign to challenge the ethics and accountability of global cell phone companies like Nokia. The socially responsible consumer will not want to miss this eye-opening film that reveals how the demand for greater communication in the globalized world is fueling one of the bloodiest wars in history.

The event will feature a discussion moderated by Roger Rouse, Professor of History at CMU!

Presented in conjunction with "Goodman Faces of Work" and supported by CAUSE (Center for African American Urban Studies and Economy) @ Carnegie Mellon University.
