Dalibor Matanic
I Love You

 

A member of the European Film Academy and a recipient of the City of Zagreb award, Mr. Matanic studied Film and Television directing at the Academy of Drama in Zagreb . From 1996 to 2000 he worked on many award-winning documentaries, including Derby , Bag, Good Luck, When our Music Roars , the short film Silence and other smaller projects. His first feature film The Cashier Wants to Go to the Seaside (2000) earned him the award for Best New Croatian Director and was the most-attended film of the year in Croatia . It also received the award for the best directorial debut in Cottbus , Germany and opened the New Directors/New Films Festival in New York City . The Drought , the first of six short films about the intimate lives of girls worldwide, won the prize for Best Short Film in Croatia . It has also won many national and international awards and was shown at Quinzaine des Realisateurs in Cannes 2003. In the opinion of many critics, Matanic's Fine Dead Girls is the best Croatian film to appear in the last fifteen years. The film deals with life in contemporary Zagreb and the problems of post-communist Croatia . The film won seven prizes at the Croatian national Film festival (including the Grand Prize) and was selected as the Croatian candidate for the Academy Awards in the foreign film category. It received the Special Jury Award in Sochi and the Grand Prix by the young-people's jury in Geneva . His latest film I Love You has thus far earned three awards and will make its American Premiere at Carnegie Mellon's "Faces of Democracy" film festival on Saturday, December 9 th , at 3pm. A second screening will take place on Sunday, December 10 th at 3pm. Dalibor Matanic will give an introduction and lead a discussion for both screenings at the South Side Works Cinema.
