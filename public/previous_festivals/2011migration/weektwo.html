Pink Saris (UK/India, 2010)
Friday, March 25, 7:30 p.m. -- Kelly Strayhorn Theater, 5941 Penn Ave

"PINK SARIS is easily one of the most interesting, relevant and fascinating documentaries I have ever seen." --London Film Festival

Indian food reception, compliments of Coriander, and panel discussion with Jui Joshi of the Women and Girls Foundation and Professor Neil Doshi of the Department of French Languages and Literatures at the University of Pittsburgh to follow the film.

Neukölln Unlimited (Germany, 2010)
Saturday, March 26, 5:30 p.m. -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University

"The rapid-fire pace, lively beats and animation sequences take Neukölln Unlimited beyond the usual clichés about immigrants and problem neighborhoods."

Featuring a breakdance performance by Abro Straughter and a reception with samplings of German and Lebanese dishes courtesy of Hofbräuhaus and Aladdin's Eatery to precede the film.
Director Agostino Imondi will host a Q&A video-conference live from Germany, via Skype, after the film!
Moderated by Randall Halle, Professor of German Film and Cultural Studies at The University of Pittsburgh

Coming up in Week Three!

Gitmek: My Marlon and Brando (US/Japan, 2010)
Thursday, April 7, 7:00 p.m. -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University

"I realized that the media never showed what was really going on in the country or the world...I wanted to change that, and make something that told the story of ordinary folks like us and not far-fetched characters living in a bubble." --Huseyin Karabey, Director

Event preceded by reception featuring food from Allegro Hearth Bakery.

Cuban Shorts: The Daily Experience of Living
Thursday, April 7, 7:30 p.m. -- Future Tenant Gallery, 819 Penn Ave

Away from the influence of a capitalist market; undergoing 40 years of U.S. economic embargo; and still under the administration of a communist regime; Cubans live in an extreme and unique social situation known only to those who have visited the island. Despite of its isolated and fragile reality, Cuban culture remains vital.

Moderated by Andres Tapia-Urzua, Department Chair, Digital Filmmaking & Video Production / Visual Effects at The Art Institute of Pittsburgh.

Five years since its inception, the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival continues to celebrate the art of filmmaking and the themes that define our contemporary social landscape. Through the power of independent film, The Carnegie Mellon Film Festival: Faces of Migration brings the intricacies surrounding migration to life. By focusing attention on unique individuals caught up in constantly shifting circumstances, these films both move us and enrich us by helping us better comprehend the current issues surrounding modern migration.
