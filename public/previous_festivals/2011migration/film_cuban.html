Our Common Experience of Living: Cuban Shorts

April 7, 7:30 PM at Future Tenant Gallery

Moderated by Andres Tapia-Urzua, Department Chair, Digital Filmmaking & Video Production / Visual Effects at The Art Institute of Pittsburgh.
Presented in conjunction with the Art Institute of Pittsburgh and Vibrant Pittsburgh.
Followed by reception featuring samplings of Cuban dishes, courtesy of Black Bean Cuban Cuisine.

Away from the influence of a capitalist market, undergoing 40 years of U.S. economic embargo, and still under the administration of a communist regime, Cubans live in an extreme and unique social situation known only to those who have visited the island. Despite its isolated and fragile reality, Cuban culture remains vital. This essence can be seen through the work of artists whose original expressions always interpret the "human condition" at the center of their poetic imagination. Culture is also the space where a critical view of the world is debated in search for a renewed cultural identity. Following this tradition, and looking to transcend cultural barriers, this selection of videos offers creativity and meaning to the daily experience of living.
