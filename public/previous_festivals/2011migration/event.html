Immigrant Nation!: The Battle for the Dream

Presented by the 2011 Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival: Faces of Migration

April 8, 4:00 PM at 105 College Hall, Duquesne University
April 9, 6:30 PM at McConomy Auditorium,
Carnegie Mellon University

Special director's appearance!

We welcome Director Esau Melendez to speak about his 2009 film, Immigrant Nation!: The Battle for a Dream. The issues of immigration and legal status are so prevalent in discussion the world over. Upon witnessing the compelling scenes that feature giant street protests, heart wrenching interviews with displaced families, and the political dramas displayed on national news networks, the viewer begins to apply a human face to one of the most controversial issues of the decade. Come watch the stories behind the issues. Then join us for a panel discussion moderated by Esau Melendez and reception featuring food from Mexico City!

What is a dream? How can a dream become enacted? How does migration affect dreams? These are all questions that the Pittsburgh Latino youth group, Jóvenes Sin Nombres (Youths Without Names), seek to answer in their feature short film debut, "Dream Acts: Sueños En Vilo," a collaboration with visual artist Felipe Castelblanco. This vibrant group of young adults selected this theme in order to capture the wide range of possibilities that could result from the impending DREAM Act legislation. Artfully divided into three distinct acts, the film illuminates the present as well as the idealized status of immigration reform in the United States. Highlighting the esoteric, the hopeful, and the unknown, Jóvenes Sin Nombres effectively reveal a series of their dreams "en vilo," or "in suspense." In a time when immigration reform in the United States could not be more uncertain, "Dream Acts: Sueños En Vilo" personifies the equally borderline state of the vivid individual and collective dreams valued by Latino youths.

Closing Night: Diplomat

Presented by the 2011 Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival: Faces of Migration

April 10, 7:00 PM at Southside Works Cinema

Special director's appearance!

Another Faces year closes with another exciting event! Director Dana Goren will appear to introduce her 2009 film, Diplomat, and then host a Q & A session with the audience. Diplomat tells the story of the famed Hotel Diplomat, once a luxury resort, now the home of 600 immigrants from the former Soviet Union. Never having integrated into Israeli society, its residents have created their own little island, secluded from the outside world. Many of them never leave the haven of its walls. We will end the evening and the year with our closing night reception, a celebration of new audiences, thought-provoking films, and unique events.
