Placebo

Directed by Abhay Kumar | 2014 | India | 96 min.



"As the camera starts infiltrating this complex mindscape of ambition and restless youth, a startling new reality begins to emerge- one in which an implosion is taking place." MOI Fight Club

Ambition is the "Ecstasy" of work. Like any drug, ambition in the workplace detaches the mind from reality to focus on the fast track to success. In Placebo, reality is an infection permeating the hallways of one of India's finest medical schools. By entering this closed world, Kumar's camera follows the training some of the best minds in India and lifts the lid on a world characterised by insurmountable pressure. Armed only with consumer Apple products and simple hand cameras, Kumar has created a film that dives deep into the issue of academic pressure that is felt around the world. The film focuses on four students and the monsters they battle as they question, on a plane of profound brilliance and elitism, could there ever be a cure?

A hybrid documentary form, Placebo freely mixes interviews, some terrific animation sequences, memories and even science fiction/fact to conjure up a potent feeling of dread which pervades the student campus. Placebo is the real-world counterpart to Aamir Khan's 2009 film on social pressures to achieve academic brilliance in India, Three Idiots.

Awards

  • International Documentary Film Festival of Amsterdam 2014, First Appearance Documentary Competition

Director

Producer, writer, director and cinematographer Abhay Kumar's first feature-length film has been called "a fiercely inventive documentary" and "a noteworthy debut that we can add to the expanding catalogue of new wave Hindie cinema." Placebo is India's first collaborative documentary film, which has been actively seeking supporters on the international platform Indiegogo and Indian platform Wishberry to enable people to contribute in the making of the project and "joining in on the process of change."
