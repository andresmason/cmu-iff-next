The Mighty Angel

Directed by Wojciech Smarzowski | 2014 | Poland | 110 min.



"The film applies itself to tracking this slow fall, nourished by temptations, a desperate and sometimes tragic struggle to survive, between despair and a glimmer of hope, which finally appears." Dorota Hartwich, Cineuropa

Based on Jerzy Plich's award-winning novel of the same title, The Mighty Angel is shocking story about alcohol addiction and one writer's attempts to overcome it. Jerzy is a writer and a heavy drinker, whose life parallels those of his Beatnik heroes, Allen Ginsburg and Jack Kerouac – those creative geniuses who preserved an era in literature while scorching their souls with any mind-altering substance in pill or liquid form. Jerzy is an author at the peak of his fame, interviewed on radio and television, idolized by students at lectures he gives.  He falls in love with a young girl and finally feels that he has the person and the reason to live for.  

We meet him at the point when he believes that he can beat his addiction. But Jerzy succumbs when he's alone one evening... He has a quick one at "The Mighty Angel" tavern and starts drinking heavily. What follows: convulsions, vomiting. Jerzy ends up in a hospital's intensive care unit. He finds that escaping from his dependence to be extremely hard. The film is a story of despair, hope, another downfall, great determination, and the light at the end of the tunnel in the form of reciprocated love to a woman who is the last chance for the return to normal life. But while sobriety can save a relationship, can the creative process survive sobriety?

Awards

  • Polish Film Awards 2015, 4 nominations including Best Screenplay, Best Actor, Best Supporting Actor, and Best Supporting Actress
  • Tokyo International Film Festival 2014, Best Actor Award (Robert Wieckiewicz) and Grand Prix Nominee

Director

Polish screenwriter and director Wojciech Smarzowski studied Filmmaking at Jagiellonian University in Kraków, and has several feature films to his credit, including the highly-acclaimed Rose (2011) and Traffic Department (2013). He has also directed many commercials and music videos.
