Fair Play

Directed by Andrea Sedláčková | 2014 | Czech Republic | 100 min.



"Sedláčková... excels at capturing the grim, strained atmosphere of the time, where people are forced to lie, collaborate and act against their better judgment and principles in order to keep their jobs or protect their loved ones." Variety

How far would you go to realize your dreams? In Fair Play, set in Czechoslovakia c. 1983, talented sprinter Anna is putting her eighteen years of training on the line for a shot at next year's Olympic Games in Los Angeles. Anna's trainers place her in a secret "special care" program, administering medical enhancements to boost her performance. A polished coming-of-age drama, Fair Play tells the intimate story of Anna’s and her mother's personal struggle between honesty and freedom.

Awards

  • Czech Lion Awards 2015, 15 nominations including Best Film, Best Director, Best Actor, and Best Actress
  • Chicago International Film Festival 2014, Audience Choice Award nominee
  • Karlovy Vary International Film Festival, Crystal Globe nominee

Director

Andrea Sedláčková is the director and screenwriter of Czech film Fair Play. She has directed, written, and edited several documentaries and films including Welcome (2008), which was nominated for a César, and Joyeux Noël (2005), which was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film. Her latest feature, Fair Play, was selected as the Czech Republic's 2015 Oscar entry for the Best Foreign Language Film Award and received 15 nominations for the 2015 Czech Lion Awards.

About Take Care

Directed by Jeremy Braverman | 2015 | USA | 16 min.

A "cancer comedy" about a theater undergrad and aspiring actress taking care of her terminally ill father, a charming, fast-talking schemer left without health insurance or a support network when his health goes south. When he realizes the demands of his care are forcing her to give up on her dreams, he tries to do the right thing--not exactly his strong suit. This character-rich dramatic comedy finds humor and grace in the most difficult of circumstances.
