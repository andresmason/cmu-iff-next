10 Minutes

Directed by Lee Yong-Seung | 2014 | Korea | 93 min.



"Lee's debut feature is a tale of contemporary precarity and economic survival." Oscar Moralde, Slant

Do these people ever go to work? Films often intend to reflect our realities, yet in so many, the world of the story exists completely outside the intense demands of the typical workplace. Lee Yong-Seung's film 10 Minutes breaks through this fantasy and elucidates the ennui and ordinariness of contemporary office life â€“ in Korea and elsewhere.

Meet Ho Chan. He works around the clock to impress his bosses. He hopes to rise from an intern to a full time employee, yet says he wants "an easy life." The only thing standing in his way? Office politics-- the backstage bickering, finger-pointing, and name-dropping constitute the true dynamic, but as the sole supporter of his family, Ho Chan must not let the office drama interfere with his work. Pressure and politics-- the two forces that drive each of us to question, during a long shift or a bad week, what is the point? This film's honesty on the matter might offer some insight into the reasons we work.

Director

South Korean Investment Executive, Producer, Screenwriter and Director Lee Yong-Seung studied film at Chung-ang University in Seoul. 10 Minutes is his first feature film.
