The 2011 Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival: Faces of Migration

presents

Our Common Experience of Living: Cuban Shorts

Away from the influence of a capitalist market; undergoing 40 years of U.S. economic embargo; and still under the administration of a communist regime; Cubans live in an extreme and unique social situation known only to those who have visited the island. Despite of its isolated and fragile reality, Cuban culture remains vital. ...more

Moderated by Andres Tapia-Urzua, Department Chair, Digital Filmmaking & Video Production / Visual Effects at The Art Institute of Pittsburgh.
Followed by reception featuring samplings of Cuban dishes, courtesy of Black Bean Cuban Cuisine.
Presented in conjunction with the Art Institute of Pittsburgh and Vibrant Pittsburgh.

Thursday, April 7 at 7:30 p.m.
Future Tenant Gallery
819 Penn Ave.

Regular Admission: general $7 | $4 student
Tickets available at the door or at cmu.edu/faces.

Five years since its inception, the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival continues to celebrate the art of filmmaking and the themes that define our contemporary social landscape. Through the power of independent film, The Carnegie Mellon Film Festival: Faces of Migration brings the intricacies surrounding migration to life. By focusing attention on unique individuals caught up in constantly shifting circumstances, these films both move us and enrich us by helping us better comprehend the current issues surrounding modern migration.
