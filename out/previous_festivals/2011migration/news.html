Festival partners with student groups for Japan relief drive


A massive 9.0 magnitude earthquake hit the Pacific Ocean near Northeastern Japan on March 11.

The fourth largest earthquake in human history and succeeding 50-100-feet tsunami caused devastating damages on this area; the number of deaths is 5,178 and the number of missing is 8,913 (as of March 16 at 11pm EST). Furthermore, people in Japan are still facing the fear of nuclear plant meltdowns as well as quakes.

In a partnership with the 2011 Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival, student volunteers from Tepper School of Business, Heinz College, and CMU JSA (Japanese Student Association) are launching a fundraiser to support the victims of this disaster. They will provide hand-made bookmarks with a traditional "Origami" crane and other goods to raise donations for the Japanese Red Cross.
