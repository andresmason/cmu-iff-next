Through five video cameras, Palestinian farmer Emad Burnat captures different angles of the developing story of his village's resistance to Israeli oppression. Set in the Palestinian village of Bil'in, Emad initially records home videos of his son's early years on camera, but gradually also begins to document a resistance led by two of his best friends against a separation barrier that has been constructed in the village. As Emad's son, Abreel, grows from infant to preschooler, the peaceful acts of protests against the dividing wall start to intensify. The struggle over the barrier soon begins to impact his daily life as night raids scare Emad's family, and as he, his friends, and his brothers are either shot at or arrested. 5 Broken Cameras depicts Emad's intensely powerful personal document of his villageÃ¢â‚¬â„¢s growing struggle against violence and oppression, with each five of his cameras being destroyed one-by-one during the resistance.




Emad Burnat is a Palestinian villager and the subject of the film, who began filming his son’s childhood and ended up recording the turmoil going on around him in his village. He has also spent time as a freelance journalist for Al-Jazeera. His other works include the documentaries Palestine Kids and Interrupted Streams.



Guy Davidi is a filmmaker and film teacher from Israel who began experimenting with film in high school. His accomplishments include his first feature film premiering at the 2010 Jerusalem International Film Festival.
