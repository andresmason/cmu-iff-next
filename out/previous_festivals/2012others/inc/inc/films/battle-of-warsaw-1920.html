The Battle of Warsaw 1920the first Polish feature-length film to be entirely in 3D, reenacts the historically significant battle of the Polish-Soviet War of 1920. The film intertwines two narrative lines: the true story of the epic defeat of the Soviet Red Army by the Polish Army and the fictional but deeply personal love story of a young actress, Ola, and a poet and cavalry officer, Jan. The film boasts skillfully-crafted sets, detailed costumes, and powerful music as it explores war, politics and, more importantly, the prices of war—personal tragedy, treachery, loss, and death.

Jerzy Hoffman, a prominent Polish film director and screenwriter, was born in Krakow, Poland, in 1932. In 1955, Hoffman graduated from the State Film Institute in Moscow with a degree in film directing. During the beginning of his directing career, he worked together with the Polish director Edward Skarzewski, and together they created twenty-seven documentary films and three feature films. Hoffman started his independent directing career in 1966. Currently, his most famous works are The Deluge (Potop, 1974), which was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Foreign Picture, and With Fire and Sword (Ogniemi mieczem, 1999), the most financially successful film production in the history of Polish cinema.
