The 2012 Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival: Faces of Others

FINAL WEEK!


Regular Admission: $5 seniors and students | $8 'Others'

Tickets available at the door or at cmu.edu/faces.

5 Broken Cameras (Palestine/Israel/Netherlands/France, 2011)
Thursday, April 12, 7:00 p.m. -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University

Through five video cameras, Palestinian farmer Emad Burnat captures his village's resistance to a separation barrier constructed on its land by Israeli oppression. Each five of his cameras are destroyed one by one as the peaceful acts of protest start to intensify.

The event will include a special appearance by director Emad Burnat, and a reception featuring food specialties courtesy of the East End Food Co-op, authentic Middle Eastern dishes compliments of Salem's Market & Grill, and Mexican cuisine provided by Mi Ranchito!

Mourning (Iran, 2011)
Friday, April 13, 7:30 p.m. -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University

A vibrant and hilarious road trip ensues as a deaf couple drives their young nephew, Arshia, back home to Tehran. The aunt and uncle employ low voices and demonstrative gestures to contemplate the boy's future in midst of a terrible predicament, however, their language is not as private as they believe.

Features a pre-screening reception with a performance by the Didar Persian Music Ensemble and authentic Persian cuisine courtesy of Kubideh Kitchen! A dance performance by members of the CMU Persian Student Organization will follow the screening.

Best Intentions (Romania/Hungary, 2011)
Saturday, April 14, 2:00 p.m. -- Harris Theater, 809 Liberty Avenue

Unexpectedly, Alex's mother suffers a stroke and is hospitalized at the local town hospital. Unsatisfied with his mother's condition, Alex becomes more and more hysterical as he is forced to decide whether to keep his mother in a hospital where she is comfortable or to transfer her to a better-established hospital in the city.

Short film "December" by Pittsburgh filmmaker Jose Muniain will precede the screening. The event will also feature an insightful discussion moderated by Andreea Deciu Ritivoi, Professor of English at Carnegie Mellon University, and a photo slideshow of images taken by families and children at the Ronald McDonald House at Children's Hospital.

Essential Kililng (Poland/Hungary/Ireland/Norway, 2010)
Saturday, April 14, 7:00 p.m. -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University

"Essential Killing is a ruthless, darkly funny survival movie charged with provocative political undertones"
-- Time Out London

The film screening will be preceded by short film "Dreams of an Immigrant" starring Pittsburgh actor and Essential Pittsburgh public radio host Paul Guggenheimer! Jessie Linn Ryan, a former Army E-4 in Iraq, will also make a special appearance.

Black Butterflies (Netherlands/South Africa, 2011) - Closing Night!
Sunday, April 15, 6:30 p.m. -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University

Black Butterflies tells the story of Ingrid Jonker, a celebrated South African poet who lived during the time of the Apartheid racial segregations. After her death, anti-apartheid activist Nelson Mandela quoted Jonker's poem "The Dead Child of Nyanga" in his first speech to the South African Parliament, labeling Jonker as one of the finest poets of South Africa.

Features a poetry reading by Carnegie Mellon School of Drama students. A Closing Night reception will follow the screening!

Every year, the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival challenges its participants to view the world through a myriad of multicultural lenses. In 2012, the tradition continues with this year's theme: Faces of Others. The Other often refers to strange faces, practices, and beliefs that challenge our understanding, security, and sense of normalcy, but at the same time, the Other is something that every person on this planet experiences. The festival invites you to explore the concept of the Other and join the minds of numerous national cinematic traditions. Visit us at cmu.edu/faces!
