The Cordillera of Dreams

Directed by Patricio Guzmán

Chile/France | 2020 | 84 min

Details
Showtimes

About the Film

“Contemplative, analytical and troubling, this is a nature film refracted through a historical trauma, a compilation of visual wonders that doubles as an act of remembrance.” Justin Chang, Los Angeles Times

The latest film from acclaimed documentarian Patricio Guzmán explores the mesmerizing mountain range that runs along the eastern side of Chile while relating it to the country’s long history of oppressive violence, most notably the 1973 coup that overthrew the democratically elected government of Salvador Allende and ushered in almost two decades of brutal dictatorship. Cordillera completes a trilogy of films that Guzmán has made on the relationship between Chile’s landscape, the 1973 coup, the longer trail of violence in the country, and dominant efforts to suppress memories of this violence. Numerous artists, activists, and filmmakers help chart the connections while highlighting the ways in which governments since the end of the dictatorship have continued to meet popular protests with state force. The result is a panoramic and yet deeply personal journey through a country that Guzmán once considered home.

About the Director

Patricio Guzmán was born in Santiago, Chile, in 1941. After studying film in Madrid, he returned to Chile, making four documentaries there before being forced into exile by the 1973 coup. From his base in France, he turned the extensive footage that he and his team had shot on life in Chile under Allende and on the coup as it unfolded into his globally acclaimed, three-part film, The Battle of Chile (1975, 1977, 1979). He went on to make other widely praised documentaries including In God’s Name (1987), Chile, Obstinate Memory (1997), and Salvador Allende (2006). He launched his trilogy on the interplay of landscape, history, and the politics of memory in Chile in 2010 with the extraordinary Nostalgia for the Light and continued it in 2015 with The Pearl Button. The Cordillera of Dreams brings the series to a close.
