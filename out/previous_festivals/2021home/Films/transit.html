Transit

Directed by Christian Petzold

Germany/France | 2018 | 101 min

Details
Showtimes

About the Film

“… a superbly deft drama… The strangeness of this story will live in your bloodstream like a virus.” Peter Bradshaw, The Guardian

What does it mean to be forced from your home and left anxiously uncertain about where, when, or even whether you’ll be able to resettle? Transit tells the story of Georg, a German Jew, fleeing advancing fascist forces, first to Paris and then to Marseille. In the port city, he meets people from a wide variety of places and backgrounds, all desperate to escape. They include Marie, a beguiling woman who will complicate his plans to leave. For the stranded characters, it’s difficult to know who to trust in a world where people, their identifying documents, and the stories that they tell don’t always match. And for the audience, it’s difficult to know when all of this is taking place. While drawing heavily on Anna Seghers’ 1944 novel, set in 1942, Petzold conflates markers of the Nazi past with many features of the present. It’s a powerful way of prompting us to think hard about the relationship between former horrors, the fate of displaced groups today, and the broader dangers posed by the resurgence of the far right.

About the Director

Born in 1960, Christian Petzold studied German philology and theater at the Free University of Berlin and subsequently studied directing at the German Film and Television Academy of Berlin. After making several well-received TV films, he released his first theatrical feature, The State I Am In, in 2000 and, over the next decade, made a succession of acclaimed features and TV pieces. In 2012, he launched what he calls his “Love in the Time of Oppressive Systems Trilogy” with Barbara and followed it in 2014 with Phoenix. Both won multiple awards at festivals around the world. Transit is at once a self-standing film and the final part of this thematic series.
