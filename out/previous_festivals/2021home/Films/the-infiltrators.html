The Infiltrators

Directed by Cristina Ibarra & Alex Rivera

USA | 2019 | 95 mins

Details
Showtimes

About the Film

“Timely, captivating and terrifying due to its real-world roots, "Infiltrators" has all the strengths of an entertaining prison break flick, with harrowing and humane traits of an uncompromising and gritty documentary.” Paul Carlson, Escape into Film

The Obama administration significantly increased immigrant deportations without trial, often sending detainees to await their fate in private detention centers run for profit. What were conditions like in these centers? And what, if anything, could be done to prevent the deportations of detainees and the forced break-up of their families and homes? In 2012, three undocumented members of the National Immigrant Youth Alliance, a group of activists known for challenging deportations, bravely volunteered to enter and be held in one of these facilities. Co-directors Ibarra and Rivera use a highly inventive mix of documentary footage and recreated scenes to chart what happened to the infiltrators and what they were able to achieve, with powerful lessons for the troubled situation that prevails today.

About the Director

Alex Rivera is a filmmaker who’s been telling ground-breaking Latinx stories for more than twenty years. His first feature film, Sleep Dealer, a cyberpunk thriller set in Tijuana, Mexico, won multiple awards at Sundance and was screened around the world.

Cristina Ibarra is a documentary filmmaker. She was born into a middle class Mexican immigrant family, and went on to study at the University of Texas at Austin. She is a member of the National Association of Latino Independent Producers, and a founding member of Fulana, a Latina multi-media collaborative, and SubCine, the first Latino self-distribution collective.
