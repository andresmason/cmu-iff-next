A single mother is restless with the grind of her city life.  As the radio references global pop culture, she escapes quietly to the Chilean countryside to visit the father of her child and the environment which threatens to disappear.  When asked why she has come after ten years of separation, her only justification is “I wanted to see you.”

Sergio Castro San-Martín

Sergio began his film career as an audio-visual producer in the Cinema School of Chile. His short films and documentaries have been selected for the Valdivia International Cinema Festival, In Edit Festival and for the International Panorama component of the International Festival of Schools of Cinema held in Buenos Aires, Argentina.  In 2007, he received funding from the Audio Visual State Fund for the development of his first feature Paseo.  He currently works as Film Teacher in addition to his role as a director and editor with Victoria Films and Promedia.
