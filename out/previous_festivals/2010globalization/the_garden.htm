Fourteen acres of farmland in the grain belt is an increasingly endangered entity.  Fourteen acres of farmland in Los Angeles is an anomaly.  Yet amongst the urban blight and memories of the 1992 L.A. riots, the South Central Farmers maintained an urban garden until bulldozers threatened to demolish their community.  Why was the land sold so cheaply to a wealthy developer in a closed-door session of the LA City Council? The city answered, “The garden is wonderful, but there is nothing more we can do.”  The mostly Latin American community of farmers responded with “Si se puede!  There is something we can do!” .

Scott Hamilton Kennedy

Kennedy’s first entered filmmaking when he majored in theater with a concentration in directing at Skidmore College.  After graduating, Kennedy went on to establish himself as a director of music videos, commercials, and promos for clients ranging from Tony Bennett to CBS, and Mattel.  His debut documentary, Our Town, earned international recognition through festival circuits, was short listed for an Oscar nomination and received a nomination for Best Documentary from the Independent Spirit Awards.
