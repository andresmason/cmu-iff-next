Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival Explores "Faces of Globalization," April 6 - April 11

Faces of Globalization, the 2010 Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival, organized by The Humanities Center presents fourteen, contemporary, award-winning films from more than eighteen countries, in addition to an international short film competition, director appearances, panel discussions, local catering, live musical performances, video conferencing technology and a special screening in conjunction with the UN World Environment Day- all at recession rates!

In Service: Iraq to Pittsburgh (USA/Iraq, 2008)
Tuesday, April 6, 7:15 p.m. McConomy Auditorium, CMU University Center
Reception sponsored by Chipotle.
"Ordinary humanity amid extraordinary events." -–Pittsburgh Post Gazette
With the special appearance of director Ralph Vituccio and Dan Connery, one of the soldiers featured in the film.
The Garden (USA, 2007)
Wednesday, April 7, 7:15 p.m. The Kelly-Strayhorn Theater, 5941 Penn Ave
Reception sponsored by East End Food Co-Op and Allegro Hearth Bakery.
"[John] Locke haunts every twist and turn in this David-and-Goliath tale about the fight to save a 14-acre community garden in one of the grittiest parts of South Los Angeles." --The Washington Post
Director Scott Hamilton Kennedy is scheduled to appear. Featuring panel by Dr. Stanley Kabala and Dr. Michael Irwin.
Malls R Us (Canada, USA, U.K., Japan, Poland, France, Dubai, 2008)
Thursday, April 8, 6:00 p.m. McConomy Auditorium, CMU University Center
"Prowls the hallowed, paradoxically light-filled yet claustrophobic temples of commerce and addresses issues of design, socialization, the losers and the winners... an engaging globe-trotter of a doc." --The Chicago Tribune
Moderated by Dr. Omer Akin, School of Architecture at Carnegie Mellon University.
Paseo (Chile, 2009)
Thursday, April 8, 8:00 p.m. McConomy Auditorium, CMU University Center
"A beautiful and intimate film." --Diego Batlle, Otros Cines
Best Chilean Film of 2009.
With an introduction by faculty members of Digital Filmmaking and Production Department of The Art Institute of Pittsburgh: Andrew Maietta, Kaz Rahman and Andres Tapia–Urzua.
The Garden (USA, 2007)

The Inheritors (Mexico, 2009)
Friday, April 9, 6:00 p.m. McConomy Auditorium, CMU University Center
"An impressive depiction of the bleak lives of Mexico's rural population... reminiscent of the early socio-critical works of directors such as Jean Rouch and Fernando Birri." --Berlin International Film Festival
Yodok Stories (Norway, 2008)
Friday, April 9, 8:00 p.m. McConomy Auditorium, CMU University Center
Reception following the event with kimbab, food provided by Ginza and food made by Korean students.
"Imaginatively peers deep inside North Korean communism to reveal a social system based on a brand of insanity that will make even hardcore anti-commies blanch." --Robert Koehler, Variety
Panel Discussion following the film with distinguished guests: Dr. Attilio Favorini, Seunghwan Shin, and Dr. Rev. Timothy Son.
Z32 (Israel/France, 2008)
Saturday, April 10, 7:15 p.m. McConomy Auditorium, CMU University Center
Reception sponsored by Allegro Hearth Bakery.
"This unconventional picture is perfect festival fare and though its hard ironic edge may mean the film does not have the same cathartic effect as [Waltz with] Bashir, it should find an art house niche internationally.” --Dan Fainaru, Screen Daily
Opening with a live performance of music, from the film score, by the multi-talented vocalist from Brazil, Lilly Abreu, and her guitarist, Eric Susoeff. Followed by a panel discussion with Helen Gerhardt, Barry Fish, Naftali Kaminski and Barry Paris, and a reception.
