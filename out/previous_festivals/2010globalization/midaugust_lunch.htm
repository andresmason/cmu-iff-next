Directed by Gianni di Gregorio; Italy, 2009; 76 min

With the help of local Italian restaurant Stagioni, the film comes to life with a reception featuring the dishes and wine, Fontana Candida from Frascati, highlighted in the film.

Pittsburgh Premiere

"The beauty of this picture is that it doesn't shy away from depicting the indignities of growing old- not the least of which is that none of your children wants to look after you- and yet manages to invest all of its elderly characters with dignity."-Toby Young, The Times Online

Director Gianni Di Gregorio explained the impetus for his role in the film with his statement, “I played the leading role because while I was explaining to the crew that we needed to find a middle-aged man, more or less an alcoholic, who had lived for years with his mother, I realized that all eyes were turned to me.” His alcoholic role is that of Gianni, a bachelor caring for his widowed mother.  When the bills are beyond his dwindling budget, he barters geriatric care for waved bills.  The old women are lonely and vulnerable in a world that is constantly moving, but when the women discover their common lot, they make a connection that is a youthful celebration of culture and cuisine!

Gianni Di Gregorio

After a childhood in the Roman cinemas, Di Gregorio abandoned literature studies for the Accademia di Arti Sceniche, where he took a diploma in directing and acting.  Seeing Scorsese’s film Mean Streets inspired him to leave the theatre and work in film as an assistant director. His transition to screenwriting was Carefree Giovanni, winner of the Gran Prix du Juri at the festival of Annecy.  His directorial collaboration with Matteo Garrone began in 2000 with Roman Summer, and continued with The Embalmer, First Love and Gomorrah,which won the Grand Prix and the Palm D'or awards at the 2008 Cannes Film Festival.
