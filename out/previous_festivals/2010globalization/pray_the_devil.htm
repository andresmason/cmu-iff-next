Before Americans were celebrating progress, as represented by the race between a black man and a woman, a woman took to a podium and boldly pledged to steer her country toward reform. Before her, a crowd of women stood in solidarity, for they had not only brought this woman to presidential power, but they had brought peace to their war ravaged nation. These were the Liberian women who held hands for peace and prayed the devil of war go back to Hell. These were the women who shed their religious differences for a greater cause, and this is the documentary that follows their indefatigable quest for peace.

Gini Reticker

Reticker started her career as an editor on renowned documentaries such as Michael Moore’s Roger & Me; Deborah Shaffer’s Emmy-nominated Fire From the Mountain; and The Awful Truth: The Romantic Comedy, for the PBS American Cinema Series.  She is one of the leading filmmakers on women’s issues.  She produced and directed the 2005 Emmy Award-winning documentary Ladies First for the PBS series Wide Angle, which focused on the role of women in rebuilding post-genocide Rwanda.

Abigail E. Disney

Along with her husband, Pierre Hauser, Abigail is co-Founder and co-President of the Daphne Foundation, a progressive, social change foundation that makes grants to grassroots, community-based organizations working with low-income communities in New York City. Since 1991, the Daphne Foundation has made millions of dollars in grants in areas ranging from women’s rights to AIDS advocacy, children’s health, labor conditions, incarceration and community organizing. She has been involved in the production of a number of documentaries with social themes.  Pray the Devil Back to Hell is her first feature film.
