ABOUT THE FILM

“Running the gamut from social comedy to actioner to war move, Clash is an original, often quite disturbing experience to watch... it will be remembered as one of the most telling depictions of modern Egypt yet filmed.”

- Deborah Young, The Hollywood Reporter


Taking place during the turmoil following the ousting of Egyptian president Morsi in 2013, Clash is set entirely inside a police truck. The detainees inside consist of Muslim Brotherhood, pro-army supporters, and those who identify as neither. Together, they must navigate their diverging political and religious backgrounds to survive this claustrophobic nightmare while violent protests rage outside.

ABOUT THE DIRECTOR

As an award winning writer and director, Mohamed Diab's work often focuses on crucial issues in Egyptian society. He is known for his debut film, Cairo 678 (2010), and for writing The Island (2007), which represented Egypt in the 2007 Oscar race. Diab's active engagement and public participation in the 2011 Egyptian Revolution led to his second feature, Clash (2016).
