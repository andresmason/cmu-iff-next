The line forms here for a new name. The line forms here for a new birth certificate. The line forms here for a new address, including the town's new name. Just like that an old identity is erased and a new identity is forged. These are values that we take for granted, rights to our identity. Something as basic as a name, is a freedom not to be taken for granted. For if a name is replaced, what is next? A style of dress? A religion? A language? A homeland? These are the unimaginable, painful stages of ethnic cleansing. Against this back drop, a contradictory love story emerges between a Muslim-Turk woman struggling to maintain her identity and a non-Muslim Bulgarian man who is charged with the task of regenerating her. However, her identity is one he is unable to erase from his mind.

Director Bio: Radoslav Spassov

Bulgarian cinematographer and director Radoslav Spassov was born in 1943. He graduated from VGIK (Moscow) in 1971, with a degree in cinematography. One of Bulgaria 's most distinguished cinematographers, he has shot more than twenty films, working with some of Bulgaria 's best directors, including: Georgi Djulgerov, Eduard Sachariev, Ivan Andonov, Georgi Stoyanov, Kiran Kolarov, Rangel Valchanov and Ludmil Staykov. He debuted as director in 1993, with A Day of Forgiveness.
