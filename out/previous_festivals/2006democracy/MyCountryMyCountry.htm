www.mycountrymycountry.com

Awards and Nominations
  • Nominated for an Independent Spirit Award for Best Documentary
  • On short list for contention in Academy Award for Best Documentary
  • Winner of the Full Frame Documentary Film Festival's Inspiration Award
  • Winner of the Flahertiana Film Festival's Award for Best Long Documentary

My Country, My Country shows the ethnic, religious, and cultural issues surrounding the 2005 Iraq elections. The film showcases Dr. Riyadh, a Baghdad resident running in the elections. He must choose between promoting the new democracy by campaigning or staying loyal to the Sunni party by boycotting the elections. He seeks the opinions of his family, friends, neighbors and fellow countrymen. Their words show the true feelings of the upcoming elections. Director Laura Poitras gives a direct yet complete picture of an election plagued by disdain for the U.S. occupation, uncertainly over the legitimacy of the elections, fear and hope for the future. Ms. Poitras will be attending the festival to introduce her film.

Director Bio: Laura Poitras

Laura Poitras studied filmmaking at the San Francisco Art Institute and the New School and dabbled in the culinary world before making films. A current New York resident, Ms. Poitras is director and producer of My Country, My Country (2006), and Flag Wars (2003). She received a Peabody Award for Flag Wars, which was nominated for both an Emmy and an Independent Spirit Award . Her other works include Oh Say Can You See ...? (2004) and Exact Fantasy (1995).
