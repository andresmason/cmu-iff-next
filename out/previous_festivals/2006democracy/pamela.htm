Pamela Yates
State of Fear

 

Pamela Yates is one of the founding members of the Manhattan based Skylight Pictures, a film and video production company. She is also a member of the Director's Guild of America (DGA) and the Writers Guild of America (WGA). Her film experience has ranged from screenwriter to director and producer with extensive experience in feature-length documentaries, as well as commercial television. From 1993 to 1995, Yates was a producer on Michael Moore's TV Nation for NBC and Fox. She also produced and directed TAKEOVER, (Official selection, Sundance 1991). Her dedication to telling stories with social issues has often led her camera to focus on countries of Latin America in films such as Witness to War (Academy Award, 1985), about an American doctor behind rebel lines in El Salvador; When The Mountains Tremble (Special Jury Award, Sundance 1984), featuring Nobel Prize laureate Rigoberta Mench; and Nicaragua: Report From the Front. Cause for Murder , which was commissioned by the PBS international series Wide Angle (2002) explores the cost of political bravery in the lives and deaths of two young Mexican lawyers, Digna Ochoa and Marigeli Tames. Her most recent film, State of Fear: The Truth About Terrorism sheds light on the Peruvian Truth and Reconciliation Commission and the twenty year war on terror in Peru .
