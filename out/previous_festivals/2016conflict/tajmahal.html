Taj Mahal

Directed by Nicolas Saada | France/Belgium | 2015 | 91 min.








Premiere:
Sunday, March 27
7:00 pm | McConomy Auditorium | CMU University Center

Event Includes:
+ Musical performance
+ Indian food and cultural reception

 

Synopsis

Louise, an 18-year-old French girl, follows her parents to Mumbai for her father’s work. While they await the availability of their new home, they lodge in a lavish suite of the Taj Mahal hotel and begin their new lives. With plans to study photography, Louise quietly observes the colorful, bustling city of Mumbai through the lens of her camera and from her window perch above all the action. While her parents go out for dinner one evening, Louise decides to stay in the suite. When strange noises are heard in the hotel corridors, Louise pieces together the unthinkable- the hotel is under terrorist attack. Alone and trapped in the suite, her only connection to the outside world is her cell phone. On the other end of the line, her parents try to fight their helplessness and reach her. Based on a true story, Taj Mahal offers a cerebral, first-hand perspective of terrorism and its devastatingly unshakable effects.

Awards

  • Venice Film Festival, 2015, Best Film

Director Nicolas Saada

Nicolas Saada studied English before earning a Masters in American Film Noir. In 1992, he was appointed program editor for drama at TV Channel Arte. From 1993 to 1998, he worked on nearly 50 TV films, including the series Les Années lycée and Tous les garçons et les lles de leur âge. In 2004, he directed his first short film, Les Parallèles, which was nominated for a César for Best Short Film in 2005. In 2009, he directed Espion(s), which was also nominated for a César, this time in the Best First Feature Film category in 2010. He has also made various short films, including Aujourd’hui (2012), which was selected for the 2013 New York Film Festival. From 1993 to 2007, Saada hosted Nova Fait son Cinéma, a program dedicated to film music on Parisian station Radio Nova.
