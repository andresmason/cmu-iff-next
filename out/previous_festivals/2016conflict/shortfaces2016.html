The Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival is celebrating its 10th anniversary with the theme “Faces of Conflict”. As a collaborative project between Carnegie Mellon and Point Park University, this year’s Student Short Film Competition invites students from all over the world to submit original short films that show their talent in filmmaking and passionately capture the ubiquitous and intimate theme of Conflict through their own personal lens. As always, the theme is open to creative interpretations in every scope, topic, and genre. We aspire to create a dynamic environment for global perspective at the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival.

Participant: Students  |  Film Length: ~5-15 minutes
Theme: Faces of Conflict  |  Submission Deadline: March 1, 2016

We will announce the short list via Email by mid March. Films selected into the short list will be screened at Student Short Film Competition Screening Event.

Date: March 25, 2016  |  Time: 6:00pm – 8:30pm

Location: George Rowland White Theater, Point Park’s University Center


AWARDS

Second Prize: $500
Third Prize: $250
People’s Choice: $250
Honorable Mention

First Place: $15,000 Scholarship
Second Place: $10,000 Scholarship
Third Place: $5,000 Scholarship

Winners of the scholarships to the Cinema Arts Department at Point Park University must meet admissions and departmental requirements. More details available by contacting infocinema@pointpark.edu.


Guidelines

  1. Film must be created by a student.
  2. Film must be approximately 5-15 minutes in length.
  3. Film must include but is not strictly limited to the Faces of Conflict theme. Theme is open for creative interpretation.
  4. If in a language other than English, and/or if audio is in any fashion muffled, subtitles are required.
  5. Each director can submit a maximum of 2 short films.
  6. Film must not have been submitted to CMU International Film Festival Short Film Competition before.
  7. Film must be submitted prior to March 1, 2016. Failure to submit prior to the due date may result in rejection from admittance.
  8. Format:
    1. MP4 or .MOV format
    2. Use H.264 codec
    3. Code Rate 5M per second IV. Video Size 720p (1280 x 720)
  9. Submission Process:
    1. To submit your film, please email your work to faceshortfilm@gmail.com directly or via GoogleDrive.
    2. Submission title must follow: “Film Title _ Director’s FullName”
    3. Each film submission must include a submission form. Click and download it here: Student Short Film Competition Submission Form
    4. All submissions must be accompanied by a $20 film competition fee. The fee remains $20 whether the director chooses to submit one or two films.

If you have any questions, please email faceshortfilm@gmail.com.
