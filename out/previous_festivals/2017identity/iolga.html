ABOUT THE FILM

“Weinreb and Kazda, for their part, sometimes seem overly enamored of their film’s formal sobriety, with a pronounced taste for painstakingly held static shots and sonic minimalism — though their understatement also has promising rewards, as in a perfectly pitched final tableau of agonized domestic stability in the wake of tragedy.”

- Guy Lodge, Variety

As a young Czechoslovakian woman, like many young adults Olga feels like an outsider. But at the age of twenty-two, her paranoia, anxiety, and self-isolation pushes her to do the unthinkable. Based on the true story of Olga Hepnarova, the last woman executed in Czechoslovakia, this film borrows from her personal journal in order to shed light on her choice to run over a crowd of pedestrians in Prague in the year 1973. In a world where the threat of terrorism has come to be the new normal, I, Olga Hepnarová gives psychological and personal insight into the mind of a killer.

ABOUT THE DIRECTORS

Czech directors, Petr Kadza and Tomas Weinreb have worked closely on films since their joint graduation from the Independent Film College in Pisek, as well as from the Film School of Academy of Performing Arts in Documentary Filmmaking and Scriptwriting. Throughout the years they have worked on several student films and feature scripts. I, Olga Hepnarova is their feature film debut.
